Research Statement

My research integrates nonlinear partial differential equations, mathematical modeling, and machine learning. I am particularly interested in numerical stability, long-time dynamics of PDEs, and the development of computational tools for data-driven scientific problems.

Recent work includes:
- Stability analysis for nonlinear Schrödinger and Klein-Gordon models.
- Numerical studies of soliton interactions and higher-order dispersion phenomena.
- Machine learning projects in image recognition, anomaly detection, and predictive analytics.

For the Visiting Faculty role, I plan to pursue projects that connect data science with mathematical theory, support student research, and collaborate with engineering and computational science colleagues.
